Same-sex marriage
My arrival in San Francisco on 
Wednesday, 4 August coincided 
with the judgment handed down 
by Chief Judge Vaughn R Walker 
of a 9th Circuit Federal District 
Court, overturning “Proposition 
8”  of  Californian  State  law,  on 
the grounds it is unconstitutional. 

Proposition 8 (or the California 
Marriage Protection Act) was a 
ballot  proposition and consti-
tutional amendment passed by 
popular vote in the November 
2008 California State elections.

Judge Walker’s conclusion was 
that proposition 8 was discrimina-
tory, as it impeded the fundamen-

marry and contravened the “equal

Amendment to the United States

its original proponents, arguing

standing to appear at the next level 
of appeal, although an injunction

of same-sex couples to marry. A 
resolution was passed calling for the 
elimination of legal barriers, such 
as proposition 8, to be removed 
“between two persons of the same 
sex who are otherwise eligible to 
marry”.
